My second and most recent internship was with Motorola Solutions in the Summer of 2022 as part of the Software Development team. Due to the coronavirus pandemic, I was a part of the internship program remotely from my home in Coppell, Texas but the internship would've taken place in Plantation, Florida.

During my time at Motorola Solutions I was able to aid the company in making a new dashboard for their users with Microsoft Power BI. This was my first time using Power BI as well as deisgning a dashboard for a company. I learned a lot through this program and I was able to broaden my knowledge about SQL Queries as well as what it feels like to work at a large company.

Power BI was a very useful tool for the company becuse it allows the users easy acces to data and information that is vital to public safety. This internship allowed me to gain further experience in the corperate world as well as work alongside established senior software developers to complete the fault data dashboard. I will be forever greatful for this internship experience.

A project I worked on during my time at Georgia Tech was an app called Care Bear. This app was designed for students to be able to log their sleep and check in on peers at Georgia Tech. My role in this app was to come up with the interface. This app was important to me because it allowed me to be able to work in a group setting with like minded peers so that we could all come together to make a useful app. This app allowed college students to track their sleep progress and share it with their friends. This app idea started as an app where friends could check on each others' sleep progress to make sure their friends were getting healthy amounts of sleep each night. This was an important app because it allowed me to demonstrate my creative freedom on the app while also making sure the app had practicality and use. Overall there are some edits I would amke to the application UI today such as varied color schemes and a cleaner UI.

Another app that I enjoyed designing was a tutoring app. This app was created with other Georgia Tech students. Through creating and designing this application I was able to to learn how to use Android Studio to design interfaces and create intuitive features such as a chat bot on the app. I was also a part of the team who worked with react to create an online chat bot which was able to answer questions that students may get stuck on. I redesigned the front end of the app using a more neutral color scheme that elicts a calming effect on the user. I have inserted images of my app and the before and after results.

An application I had the oppertunity to design during my Master's was an addition to Uber for university students. I primarily used Figma to design this app and all of its components and features.

This app allowed me to incorperate important points of knowledge from the semester such as attention retention for multiple age groups, application design, educational design, and many others into the app. Over the course of the 2021 semester, I learned how to teach users through applications, and how to keep users immersed in applications when they are using them for various purposes, such as learning. This was a unique oppertunity for me to display my UI interests because prior to this I had never worked on an eduational app, especially for a target audience who was from the middle school to the colleget level. This app was important because I was able to display my creative freedom while also learning the art of application design. The main goal of this application was to connect users to other computer science experts and help teach younger students how to network in the STEM work force.

My first internship was with Raytheon in Indianapolis in the Summer of 2018 as a part of the V-22 Osprey team. This was before it became Raytheon Technologies.

I worked primarily on supporting scripts and applications that furthered team productivity through better data munging of relevant information to exportable formats. One such example was a batch file that would determine data packet deliveries in a stream and export its associated information into a csv (Comma Separated Values) file that could be more easily parsed through for manual analysis. I also worked with WireShark and Java during the semester.

My most important project was a C# application that allowed users to export their User Stories and other units of work via Agile methodology managed through Microsoft's Team Foundation Server (TFS) into PowerPoint (.pptx) files. I heavily utilized Visual Studio as my IDE and niche C# libraries that communicated with TFS to complete this project.

During my internship, I was part of Xtern, a program that provided a full-packaged internship experience with networking events for both professional development and personal enrichment, providing me with both growth in corporate soft skills and life-long friends.
